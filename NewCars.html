  1. Body Styles: The RAV4 is available in various body styles, including a 4-door SUV and a hybrid version.

  2. Engine Options: Depending on the generation and trim level, the RAV4 may be equipped with different engine options, including four-cylinder gasoline engines and hybrid powertrains. The specific engine options and power outputs can vary by model year.

  3. All-Wheel Drive: Many RAV4 models offer all-wheel drive (AWD) or four-wheel drive (4WD) systems for improved traction and off-road capability.

  4. Interior and Cargo Space: The RAV4 typically offers a spacious interior with comfortable seating and ample cargo space. The rear seats are often foldable to increase cargo capacity.

  5. Technology: Toyota has incorporated various technological features into the RAV4 over the years, including touchscreen infotainment systems, advanced safety features, and driver-assistance systems like adaptive cruise control and lane-keeping assist.

  6. Safety: The RAV4 often comes equipped with safety features such as multiple airbags, anti-lock brakes, traction control, and stability control. More recent models also include Toyota Safety Sense (TSS), a suite of advanced safety technologies.

  7. Fuel Efficiency: The RAV4 has typically offered competitive fuel efficiency, especially in its hybrid versions, making it an attractive option for those looking to save on fuel costs.
